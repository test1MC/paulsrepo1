What Happened to My Paragraph and Character Styles?

what happened to my paragraph and character styles?

In Adobe FrameMaker, you used the Character Designer, Paragraph Designer, and Catalogs to manage your text formatting needs. When you import Adobe FrameMaker documents, all the style information is placed in a stylesheet, which can be viewed in 's Stylesheet Editor.

In addition to the styles you created in Adobe FrameMaker, you will notice many more styles that adds. These styles are adopted from W3C specifications (see http://www.w3c.org), or they are MadCap-specific styles used to support unique features in . See About Styles and Stylesheets.

You can use the following steps to locate your imported styles:

  2. Navigate to your stylesheet.

    When you add new stylesheets (not import them), the traditional default location for those stylesheets is within the Resources folder (although they can also be placed in custom folders). To open a stylesheet from this location, double-click the Resources folder. Then double-click the Stylesheets folder.

  3. Double-click the stylesheet. The Stylesheet Editor opens.

  4. In the Stylesheet Editor, your imported paragraph and character styles can be found in the following locations, depending on your selections in the Import FrameMaker Wizard.

    paragraph styles

    If you imported paragraph styles without mapping them to styles, they can be found under the p style in the Stylesheet Editor.

    example

    If you imported a paragraph style called "Chapter," you can find it under the p style, and its new full name is "p.Chapter."

    Please note that this example is showing the Advanced view of the Stylesheet Editor. You can also work in the Simplified view of the editor, which shows the styles in a grid.

         character styles

    If you imported character styles without mapping any of them to existing styles, your character styles can be found under the span style.

    example

    If you imported a character style called "Emphasis," you can find it under the span style, and its new full name is "span.Emphasis."

         mapped styles

    If you imported paragraph and character styles, mapping them to existing styles, they will be found under the tags that you mapped them to.

    example

    If you imported a paragraph style called "Chapter" and mapped it to the style called "h1," you can find the imported style under the h1 style, and its new full name is "h1.Chapter."

    example

    If you imported a character style called "Emphasis" and mapped it to the style called "b," you can find it under the b style, and its new full name is "b.Emphasis."

Why Does the User Interface Look So Different from Adobe FrameMaker?

why does the user interface look so different from adobe framemaker?

There are significant differences between the Adobe FrameMaker and user interfaces.

In Adobe FrameMaker, a window appears when you open a document. The window shows the document laid out in a page design with text, images, and other elements in their places. You can customize the interface slightly by displaying window guides such as borders, text symbols, rulers, and grids.

Adobe FrameMaker was developed in the late 1970s, while is much more modern looking and is designed to support the future of single-source publishing.

Unlike the Adobe FrameMaker interface, which presents a traditional page analog for text entry with pull-down menus for additional options, features a visual XML editor, driven by cascading stylesheets.

Editors are available for every project element and can be arranged as tabs or accordion items; docked or floating. You can open and work in multiple windows to suit your preferences. Visual indicators of conditions, variables, and index markers speed your development process.

For more information see Touring the Workspace.

What About Third-Party Tools That I Depended On to Enhance Adobe FrameMaker?

what about third-party tools that i depended on to enhance adobe framemaker?

Much of the add-on functionality that you depend on in Adobe FrameMaker is supplied by the analysis feature in Flare. The project analysis can scan your topics and make suggestions for missing variables, broken links, missing images, and many other analytical tasks. Then with a few clicks of your mouse, you can correct the issues that were identified through project analysis. For more information see About Analysis and Reports.
