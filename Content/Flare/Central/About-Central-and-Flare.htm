About MadCap Central and Flare

Introduction

Following are some of the main benefits of integrating your Flare projects with Central:

  • Host builds with custom vanity URLs
  • Assign user permissions
  • Use integrated checklists
  • Collaborate with subject matter experts and other authors
  • Create and manage tasks
  • Customize dashboards and widgets

For more complete information about Central, see its online Help:

https://help.madcapsoftware.com/central

Tasks Associated with Central

tasks Associated with Central

Following are tasks that you can perform from the MadCap Central window pane or the Source Control ribbon in Flare:

  • Log In and Log Out See Logging In and Out of Central in Flare.
  • Selecting Transfer Protocol If you plan to bind a Flare project to a MadCap Central license, you can choose between a couple of transfer protocol options when performing the binding. This includes binding new projects or importing existing projects from Central. It does not affect existing projects that are already bound. See Selecting a MadCap Central Transfer Protocol.
  • Uploading (Binding) Projects See Uploading (Binding) Projects.
  • Importing Projects See Importing Projects from Central.
  • Source Control
    • Committing and Synchronizing (Pull, Push) in Single-bound Model If you are working in a single-bound model, you author content in Flare. When you want to transfer your changes to Central, you use the Source Control ribbon to commit files that youâ€™ve changed. This gives you the opportunity to organize your files into different groups when you add them to Central, and to add a comment to each commit as well. After this, you synchronize (pull, push) your changes with the cloned project on Central. See Committing and Synchronizing (Pull, Push) in a Single-bound Model.
    • Pushing in Dual-bound Model If you are working in a dual-bound model, you author content in Flare and use your third-party source control tool to synchronize your files with those from other writers. After this, you use Flare to push those changes to Central. See Pushing in a Dual-bound Model.
  • Viewing Project Properties See Viewing Project Properties.
  • Removing Bindings See Removing Bindings from Projects.

You can also do the following if you want to use MadCap Central for your review process:

  • Send for Review See Sending Files for Review on Central.
